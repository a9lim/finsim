# Shoals — Art Prompts for Gemini Image Generation

> Disco Elysium-style portraits and flavor images for the Shoals trading sim.
> All prompts target a **painterly oil-on-canvas** look: visible brushstrokes,
> slightly impressionistic, psychologically expressive faces, muted earth tones
> punctuated by saturated accent colors. Portraits are bust/shoulder crops with
> loose, atmospheric backgrounds suggesting setting rather than depicting it.

---

## Style Preamble

Prepend this to every prompt:

> **Oil painting portrait in the style of Disco Elysium's character art.
> Visible brushstrokes, painterly texture, slightly exaggerated features that
> reveal personality. Muted background with impressionistic color washes.
> Psychologically intense expression. No photorealism. No anime. No clean
> digital rendering. Aspect ratio 3:4, bust/shoulder crop.**

For flavor/scene images, replace the last sentence with:

> **Wide establishing shot, 16:9, environmental storytelling through color
> and atmosphere rather than detail.**

## Flavor / Scene Images

### Trading Floor — Calm
> Wide oil painting of a modern trading floor bathed in blue-green monitor
> light. Early morning, coffee steam rising, a few figures at desks reviewing
> pre-market data. Floor-to-ceiling windows show a gray city skyline at dawn.
> Atmosphere of tense anticipation. Cool blues and muted greens. Painterly,
> impressionistic — the screens are color blocks, not readable text.

### Trading Floor — Crisis
> The same trading floor but saturated in angry reds and alarm yellows.
> Multiple figures standing, gesturing, one person with head in hands.
> Screens are walls of red. The dawn light through windows has been replaced
> by the harsh fluorescent of a long night. Papers scattered. The brushstrokes
> are more aggressive — chaos rendered in paint.

### Capitol Rotunda — Vote Night
> The interior of a grand legislative chamber seen from above, painted in deep
> mahoganies and warm golds. Tiny figures at desks, a speaker at the podium.
> Gallery packed with shadows. The American flag analog (Columbian flag) hangs
> behind the dais. Dramatic chiaroscuro lighting — shafts of light from high
> windows cut through institutional gloom. The weight of democracy.

### PNTH Board Room
> A stark, modern conference room with floor-to-ceiling glass overlooking a
> tech campus. Long white table, a few figures in silhouette at opposite ends —
> the Dirks-Gottlieb divide made spatial. Cold blue-whites and steel grays.
> One warm accent: amber light from a laptop screen illuminating a face.
> Corporate warfare painted like a Dutch master's study of power.

### The Continental Newsroom
> A cluttered, warm newsroom in golden late-afternoon light. Desks stacked
> with papers, coffee cups, pinboard walls covered in clippings and red
> string. A single figure bent over a laptop in the foreground, face
> illuminated by screen glow. Background figures blurred in conversation.
> Nostalgic but urgent — old-school journalism still alive, still dangerous.

### Strait of Hormuz — Oil Crisis
> Aerial view of a narrow ocean strait at sunset, painted in dramatic oranges,
> deep blues, and petroleum blacks. Tiny silhouettes of tanker ships. Smoke
> on the horizon — ambiguous, could be flare stacks or conflict. The water
> reflects fire colors. The chokepoint that controls the global economy,
> rendered as a Turner seascape.

### Federal Reserve — FOMC Meeting
> A wood-paneled conference room with a long oval table, institutional
> lighting, and the weight of monetary policy visible in every muted surface.
> Figures in dark suits around the table, one at the head — Hartley. Papers,
> water glasses, the seal on the wall. Painted in institutional blues and
> warm wood tones. Calm on the surface, but the brushstrokes vibrate with
> the tension of a rate decision that will move trillions.

### Khasurian Border — Night
> A frozen Eastern European landscape under a dark sky, painted in icy blues,
> slate grays, and the distant orange glow of military positions. Bare trees,
> snow, a road disappearing into darkness. Tiny vehicle headlights in the
> distance. The ceasefire line — quiet, but the kind of quiet that precedes
> artillery. Atmospheric, ominous, vast.

### Farsistan — Palace Interior
> An opulent palace reception hall painted in warm golds, rich crimsons, and
> marble whites. Ornate arches, geometric tilework suggested by impressionistic
> brushwork. A few robed figures in conversation. Through a window, desert
> light. Oil wealth made architectural — beautiful and unsettling. The
> hospitality is a weapon and the painting knows it.

### Little St. James — Presidential Retreat
> A tropical island estate at dusk, painted in lush greens, warm corals, and
> the deep blue of the Caribbean. A grand white colonial building glows with
> interior light. Boats at a private dock. Palm trees frame the scene. It
> should feel exclusive and slightly sinister — the place where deals are made
> away from cameras. Paradise with an agenda.

### Boliviara — Lithium Mines
> A vast open-pit mine carved into a mountainside, painted in raw earth tones —
> sienna, umber, iron oxide red. Tiny machinery at the bottom of the pit.
> Jungle creeping in at the edges. A single flag (Boliviaran) on a pole at
> the ridge. The resource that powers AI chips, dug from the earth by hand.
> Beautiful and brutal. The scale makes humans invisible.

### The Sentinel Studio
> A cable news set painted in aggressive reds, electric blues, and harsh white
> studio lighting. A single anchor figure at a curved desk, multiple screens
> behind showing graphics. The set is designed to project power and urgency.
> Everything is glossy, sharp, performative. The painting should feel slightly
> surreal — the manufactured reality of partisan media as art.

### Epilogue — Dawn Over Philadelphia
> A wide panoramic view of a city skyline at sunrise, painted in soft pinks,
> lavenders, and gold. The skyline is recognizable as a major eastern city —
> neoclassical buildings, modern towers. A river in the foreground reflects the
> sky. No people visible. The morning after everything: the election, the
> crisis, the resolution. Whatever happened, the city is still here. Quiet,
> hopeful, ambiguous. The final image the player sees.

## Generation Notes

- **Portraits**: Generate at **768x1024** (3:4) for consistent UI framing
- **Flavor scenes**: Generate at **1920x1080** (16:9) for full-width display
- **Consistency**: Re-use the style preamble verbatim. If a character drifts
  from the oil-painting look, add "thick impasto brushstrokes, canvas texture
  visible, no smooth gradients" to pull it back
- **Color temperature**: Politicians skew warm (power, wood, gold). Tech people
  skew cool (steel, blue, screen glow). Journalists are warm but messy.
  Geopolitical leaders match their region's palette
- **Expressions**: Disco Elysium portraits succeed because the faces are
  psychologically loaded. Every portrait should feel like it's mid-thought,
  mid-sentence, or mid-judgment. No neutral headshots. No smiling for the
  camera. These are people caught in the act of being themselves
